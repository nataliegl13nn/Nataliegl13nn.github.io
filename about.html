My Background

I'm a graduate student currently studying web authoring and design. I have a strong interest in storytelling, hip-hop culture, and how digital tools can make academic content more accessible to everyday audiences.

Why I Chose This Topic

I chose to focus on “Roxanne’s Revenge” because it’s a moment in hip-hop history that changed the game. Roxanne Shanté’s voice, delivery, and confidence reshaped what it meant to battle in rap — especially as a young woman in a male-dominated space. Her story deserved a place online where more people could engage with it.

What I Learned in This Course

This course helped me understand the power of clean, responsive design. I learned how to structure web pages using HTML, style them with CSS, and make my content accessible for mobile users. More importantly, I learned how to translate complex ideas into a digital format that’s easy to read and interact with.

Transforming Academic Writing

Turning a research paper into a website wasn’t just about copying and pasting. I had to break up long paragraphs, simplify my language, and think about flow and visual hierarchy. This process taught me how to communicate better — not just as a writer, but as a digital creator.
